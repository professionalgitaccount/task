Conclusions and limitations

Optimal treatment regimens for CDI were:

Fidaxomicin for non-severe initial CDI

 

Vancomycin for severe CDI

 

Fidaxomicin for first recurrence

 

Fecal microbiota transplant (FMT) for subsequent recurrence

  • Results were consistent with prior literature that supports earlier use of fidaxomicin
    (either for initial treatment of CDI or first recurrence)
  • The most effective treatments, with highest cure rates, are also cost-effective due to
    — Averted mortality
    — Utility loss
    — Costs of re-hospitalization and/or further treatments for recurrent CDI
  • Metronidazole is suboptimal for non-severe CDI as it is less beneficial than
    alternative strategies
  • Fidaxomicin and FMT should take a more prominent role in the management of
    recurrent CDI due to the cost-effective nature of these therapies

Limitations

  • Study did not stratify individuals into risk categories for recurrence, such as those with
    inflammatory bowel disease, transplant patients, or malignancy. These subgroups may have higher risk of recurrence and additional factors related to their underlying disease that would certainly affect treatment outcomes and costs
  • Study did not incorporate prior antibiotic exposure into model
  • Study did not model prophylactic vancomycin with systemic antibiotics
  • FMT was limited to second or subsequent recurrence per IDSA/SHEA guidelines
  • Input parameters for CDI proportion hospitalized, and hospital length of stay were taken from a single study, and in reality are likely highly variable
  • No direct comparisons of all therapies for second and subsequent recurrence
    were available
  • Many of the input parameters come from small studies, including some populations with inflammatory bowel disease, which may not represent the general population. Study attempted to account for this variability using the probabilistic sensitivity analysis
    and still found the results to be robust
